Chapter I

Brisbane to Zürich — The Adventure Begins

19 – 23 April 2026
Sunday 19 April — Departure from Brisbane

With bags packed, passports checked and a palpable sense of excitement, Andrew and Heather set off from Brisbane on the 19th of April, bound for Switzerland. The journey ahead was a long one — Singapore Airlines' SQ346 route carrying them north-west through the night, via a connection at Changi Airport, before the final leg deposited them wide-eyed at Zürich Flughafen on the morning of the 20th. After close to 24 hours in transit, a brisk hop on the S-Bahn into the city and a very welcome check-in at Easy Hotel on Heinrichstrasse in the hip Kreis 4 neighbourhood, they were finally, properly, in Switzerland.

Monday 20 April — Exploring Zürich

Jet lag was largely ignored on the 21st as Andrew and Heather threw themselves into the city. Zürich proved to be everything the guides promised — the Altstadt with its twin towers, the Limmat riverbanks glittering in spring sunshine, tram rides along the lake, and the trendy Kreis 4 and 5 districts right on their doorstep. Foraging for places to eat and sitting in bars watching the world go by was a lovely way to spend the day, including a short trip up the Funicular to the city hall.

Zürich, 21 April
Tuesday 21 April — Stein am Rhein & Rhine Falls

The 22nd saw Andrew and Heather venture out of the city on what became one of the highlights of their Zürich days. The morning was spent in Stein am Rhein — a medieval gem on the Rhine where the central Rathausplatz is lined with elaborately frescoed facades dating back centuries. They wandered the cobbled lanes and visited St. George's Abbey before heading west to the main event: Rhine Falls. Europe's largest waterfall didn't disappoint. Some 600,000 litres of water per second thunder over the basalt rock, sending a permanent mist drifting across the viewing platforms and a satisfying roar through the valley. Andrew had the DSLR out and made the most of it.

Wednesday 22 April — St. Beatus-Höhlen, Swiss Caves

Before Barcelona beckoned, Andrew and Heather squeezed in one more Swiss experience — the extraordinary St. Beatus-Höhlen, carved by an underground river into the steep limestone cliffs above the northern shore of Lake Thun. Legend holds these caves were once lair to a dragon, dispatched by the hermit monk St. Beatus himself. The reality — kilometre after kilometre of stalactites, underground waterfalls, and rushing subterranean streams — was dramatic enough without the mythology. It proved the perfect send-off before heading to the airport for the early morning flight to Spain the next day.

Chapter II

¡Bienvenidos a Barcelona!

23 – 27 April 2026
Thursday 23 April — Sant Jordi & Arrival

Swiss Air LX1952 deposited Andrew and Heather into warm Mediterranean sunshine on 23 April — and what a day to arrive. It was La Diada de Sant Jordi, Catalonia's beloved festival of roses and books: tradition demands that lovers exchange a red rose and a novel, and the entire city had taken the brief seriously. Every street corner was stacked with flowers; booksellers had colonised the footpaths of the Gothic Quarter. The Ajuntament de Barcelona threw open its magnificent Gothic halls for a public open day, and Andrew and Heather walked straight in. They checked into Hostal Drassanes, a ten-minute walk from the harbour and the foot of La Rambla — a superb base for everything that followed.

Friday 24 – Sunday 26 April — City Days

Three full days to absorb one of Europe's great cities. They threaded through the Gothic Quarter's labyrinthine lanes, followed the modernisme trail along the Eixample, and completed the Gaudí pilgrimage: Sagrada Família (worth every minute of the walk to get there), Casa Batlló, Park Güell. The architecture doesn't just impress — it genuinely astonishes. Evenings were spent at Barceloneta seafood restaurants where dinner never started before half nine, the wine was always cold, and the company was always good.

Chapter III

Zürich — The Family Assembles

27 April – 1 May 2026
Monday 27 April — Bella joins; John & Sue arrive from Manchester

The 27th of April was a day of beautiful reunions at Zürich Airport. Andrew and Heather flew back from Barcelona on LX1951, with Bella. She had hopped in a taxi to meet Andrew and Heather, now on their way to the airport all together. Almost simultaneously, EZY2193 touched down from Manchester carrying Andrew's parents, John and Sue, who had been watching the itinerary with excitement for weeks. All five relocated to the Viadukt Apartments on Josefstrasse — a proper flat with room to breathe, right in the same Kreis 5 neighbourhood they already knew and loved. That evening, squeezed around a table in one of the Viadukt arches, the group raised a glass to the week ahead.

Tuesday 29 April — Day Trip: Lucerne & the Lion Monument

With the Swiss Travel Pass loaded onto everyone's phones, printed and readied, the group boarded the direct train to Lucerne — 45 minutes and a world away. The Chapel Bridge with its painted medieval panels, the gleaming lakefront, and the famous Lion Monument all featured. It was Thorvaldsen's dying lion, carved directly into a cliff face, that stopped everyone in their tracks. Commemorating the Swiss Guards who gave their lives defending the French royal family in 1792, it carries a quiet, sorrowful dignity that no photograph quite captures. Mark Twain called it the most mournful piece of stone in the world. Hard to argue.

Thursday 30 April — Zürich by Tram, Train & Boat

The 30th was Zürich itself — properly and unhurriedly. The group spent the day threading through the city on its legendary public transport network: trams along the Bahnhofstrasse, the Polybahn funicular up to the ETH Terrace for city panoramas, and in the afternoon a Lake Zürich boat cruise all the way down to Rapperswil — the "Town of Roses" — 100% free on the Swiss Travel Pass. The two-hour glide along the lake, the Alps growing ever taller on the horizon, was exactly the kind of effortless, sumptuous experience Switzerland does so well.

Chapter IV

Interlaken & the High Alps

1 – 5 May 2026
Friday 1 May — The Train Journey to Interlaken

The morning of 1 May saw five very excited people boarding a direct InterCity train at Zürich Hauptbahnhof. As it wound south through the Swiss Mittelland and into the Bernese Oberland, the landscape transformed — valleys deepened, rivers ran whiter, and the mountains began asserting themselves with increasing authority. Then, all at once, the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau appeared in the train windows, their north faces filling the sky above everything else. From Interlaken Ost, a short bus ride up the hillside brought the group to their Airbnb in Goldswil bei Interlaken — a tiny hamlet perched above the valley with an uninterrupted view straight to those three famous summits.

"What an absolutely breathtaking location they had chosen. From the terrace, the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau sat there in the afternoon sun as if posing for a painting — except no painting had ever quite done them justice."

Saturday 2 May — Andrew Hikes Harder Kulm; Lunch with Laura

Andrew was up before the birds on the 2nd. While the rest of the group breakfasted at a civilised pace and then boarded the Harderbahn cog railway from Interlaken, Andrew had quietly laced up his boots and set off on foot — straight up the mountain. The Harder Kulm trail climbs nearly 900 metres through forest and open meadow, and the first photographs, time-stamped at 6:48am, show the valley still in deep shadow below while the high peaks above are already catching the season's first gold of sunrise. By the time he reached the Harder Kulm summit restaurant at 1,322 metres, the rest of the group had arrived by cog train — and there was an unexpected delight waiting: Laura, Heather's exchange student from many years ago, had made the journey out to join them. Lunch at the summit, with Lakes Thun and Brienz spread on either side far below and the full wall of the Eiger rising directly opposite, was one of those meals that nobody forgets.

Sunday 3 May — Lauterbrunnen & Mürren

The 3rd took the group into the Valley of 72 Waterfalls. A short train from Interlaken Ost brought them to Lauterbrunnen, where 300-metre cliffs rise vertically on both sides and Staubbachfall plunges off the rock face in a silver thread visible straight from the station. From there, a cable car up to Grütschalp and a narrow-gauge train across the cliff-edge delivered them to the car-free village of Mürren — one of the most dramatically situated settlements in the Alps. The walk through Mürren's narrow lanes offered the Big Three — Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau — at almost exactly eye level, which is arguable a better perspective than from the top itself. The afternoon was spent on terrace benches with coffee and a view that makes most other views feel slightly inadequate.

Monday 4 May — Jungfraujoch: the Highest Railway in Europe

The 4th of May was the big one. Up early, first train to Grindelwald Terminal, then straight aboard the Eiger Express — a massive three-cable gondola with floor-to-ceiling windows, whisking passengers up the entire face of the mountain in just 15 minutes. The view back across Grindelwald as the gondola climbed was jaw-dropping. A transfer at Eigergletscher onto the famous Jungfrau Railway — bored through the living rock of the Eiger and Mönch — brought the group to the summit station at Jungfraujoch, 3,454 metres. Above the clouds, in temperatures well below zero, they stood on the Sphinx Observatory terrace and looked out over the Aletsch Glacier, the longest in the Alps. In the Ice Palace below, rooms carved from ancient glacial ice glittered blue-white under dim lighting. It is, without question, one of the most extraordinary places on Earth that a train can take you.

"At 3,454 metres the snow was blinding white and the air was thin. The Sphinx Observatory floated above the clouds. Below in the glacier, carved corridors of ancient ice went on and on."

Tuesday 5 May — Andrew's 18km Hike; Lake Brienz by Boat

Andrew was at it again. The photographs from the morning of 5 May are time-stamped at 5:06am, showing him already on the trail from Iselwald on the southern shore of Lake Brienz, in near-darkness. What followed was an 18-kilometre hike that took him around and above the lake — through dense forest, along cliff-edge paths, and past the thundering Giessbach Falls — before he descended into the lakeside town of Brienz around midday. There, he met the rest of the group, who had taken the more sensible option of arriving by boat from Interlaken. They sat together at a waterfront restaurant for a long, well-earned lunch. The boat ride back down the turquoise lake to Interlaken, with the Brienzer Rothorn reflected in the water, completed a perfect day.

Chapter V

All Roads Lead to the Airport

6 – 7 May 2026

On the 6th of May, the group boarded the train back north to Zürich for one final night together — a flat in Seebach in the northern suburbs. It was a quieter evening, laced with the familiar mixture of deep holiday satisfaction and that particular wistfulness that descends when you know it's nearly over. Zürich, as if sensing the mood, was at its most golden.

On the morning of the 7th, all five went to Zürich Airport together. At the departures hall, the group split for the last time: John and Sue settled in for the long wait for their EasyJet EZY2194 back to Manchester, whilst Andrew, Heather and Bella checked in together for Singapore Airlines SQ345. The three Australians took off first, swapping knowing looks as the city fell away beneath them. Eighteen days. Zürich twice. Barcelona. A cave system beside a lake, the medieval streets of Stein am Rhein, the thundering Rhine Falls, the Chapel Bridge, a mountaintop at 3,454 metres, two dawn hikes, lake boats, cog railways, and more trains than any of them had ever taken before. They'd done every last bit of it — together.
